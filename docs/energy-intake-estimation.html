Chapter 9 Energy intake estimation

Figure 9.1: Gross energy content of selected feeds. Source: NANP Library

Pause and think What do you notice about the GE content of the feeds shown?

You may have noticed that these feeds split into roughly three categories: 1) very energy-dense fat supplements, 2) vitamins and minerals with zero energy, and 3) everything else.

High fat feeds have more GE than high carbohydrate or high protein feeds. Feeds with a greater ash content have lesser GE.

We can see clearly that fecal dry matter contains a fair amount of energy, because dried animal dung will readily burn.

9.0.1 3-1

\[ROM = 100 - Ash - NDF - Starch - (FA/FatFactor) - (CP - 0.64*sNPNCPE) \]

where FatFactor = 1 if Feedtype = fatty acid or FA soap and 1.06 for all other feeds. Values are a percentage of DM.

9.0.2 3-2

$$ \[\begin{align} \text{GE_DM of feed} &= (0.042 * \text{NDF_DM}) + \\ & \ \ \ \ \ (0.0423 * \text{Starch_DM}) + \\ & \ \ \ \ \ (0.040 * \text{ROM_DM} )+ \\ & \ \ \ \ \ (0.094 * \text{FA_DM}) + \\ & \ \ \ \ \ 0.0565 *( \text{CP_DM} - \text{sNPNCPE_DM}) + \\ & \ \ \ \ \ (0.0089 * \text{sNPNCPE_DM}) \\ \end{align}\] $$

Where feed fractions are expressed as a percentage of DM.
